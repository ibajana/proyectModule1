About this page

The Beautiful Game is the ultimate source for football enthusiasts seeking a comprehensive experience during the most thrilling tournament on the planet. With an intuitive and appealing design, the platform provides real-time coverage of all matches, giving users instant access to detailed statistics, line-ups, goals, and highlights.

In addition to live coverage, The Beautiful Game offers an extensive archive of news and analysis, keeping visitors updated on the latest events, exclusive interviews with players and coaches, as well as expert opinions. The multimedia sections include high-quality image galleries, exciting videos, and replays of key moments, allowing fans to relive the action at any time.

The site also stands out for providing comprehensive information about all participating teams, their star players, historical statistics, and tactical analyses. Fans can explore detailed profiles of each team, learn about players' backgrounds, and track the performance of their favorite teams throughout the tournament.

Interactivity is key at The Beautiful Game, with features that enable users to participate in polls, contests, and online discussions, creating a vibrant community of World Cup followers. Furthermore, The Beautiful Game is optimized for mobile devices, ensuring that fans can enjoy the excitement of the tournament anywhere and anytime.

In summary, The Beautiful Game offers an immersive and comprehensive experience, serving as the ultimate digital destination for those who want to fully immerse themselves in the passion and excitement of this global football event.

Records

At Russia 2018, Egypt goalkeeper Essam El-Hadary appeared as a starter in the defeat of the Pharaohs 2-1 against Saudi Arabia and became the oldest player to play a match in a World Cup at the age of 45 years and 161 days.
The Italian Paolo Maldini recorded, between the 1990 and 2002 editions of the World Cup, 2,217 minutes played.
Decades go by and the name Just Fontaine remains current in football. The Frenchman leads the ranking of top scorers in a single edition of the World Cups by scoring 13 goals in the 1958 World Cup in Sweden.
On June 26, 1954 at the Stade Olympique de La Pontaise (Lausanne), in the quarterfinals of the World Cup in Switzerland, Austria eliminated the host team with a score of 7-5: 12 goals in total
Between the 2002 and 2014 editions of the World Cup, German Miroslav Klose left some unbeatable records, including the 17 victories he achieved with the German national team, more than any other footballer in the history of the championship.
Mexican Rafael Márquez, with 17 caps, is the player with the most appearances as captain in the FIFA World Cup.
The Italian Dino Zoff, at 40 years and 134 days old, is the oldest footballer to play in a FIFA World Cup final. He achieved it with his team against West Germany in Spain 1982
The Brazilian Ronaldo and the German Miroslav Klose share the record of the player with the greatest number of games in which he scored at least one goal, with 11 games
The Portuguese Cristiano Ronaldo, who to date holds the title of greatest scorer in the history of football, became the oldest player in Russia 2018 to score a hat-trick in a match.
On ten occasions, both England's Peter Shilton and France's Fabien Barthez came away with clean sheets in World Cup games. Shilton achieved it in the 1982, 1986 and 1990 championships, while Barthez did it in the 1998, 2002 and 2006 championships.
A record that is difficult to beat in the FIFA World Cup is the one held by Brazilian legend Pelé. The King of football became champion in 1958, at just 17 years and 249 days old, the youngest in history.
